Who was Mike Spivey?

Mike Spivey, a former football player, achieved remarkable success in both college and professional football.

From 1973 to 1976, he played as a defensive back for the University of Colorado (CU) Buffaloes, specializing in the corner and “Apache” back positions.

Spivey’s exceptional speed, strength, and skill on the field earned him numerous accolades during his college career, including being named first-team All-Big Eight Conference and receiving an honorable mention All-American recognition from the Associated Press in his senior year.

Born on March 10, 1954, in Houston, Texas, Spivey attended Carver High School in Aldine before joining the University of Colorado.

He leaves behind his wife Debra Spivey, his daughter Nicole (Chris) Drzewiecki, his son Joshua (Andrea) Spivey, and their cherished puppy, Mercy.

Spivey made significant contributions to CU’s 1976 Big Eight championship team and participated in prestigious bowl games such as the Astro-Bluebonnet Bowl and the Orange Bowl.

In the 1977 NFL draft, the Chicago Bears selected Spivey in the second round.

He enjoyed a six-year career in the NFL, playing for the Bears, Oakland Raiders, New Orleans Saints, and Atlanta Falcons.

It was during his time with the Bears that Spivey experienced a transformative journey, discovering faith and engaging in Bible study, influenced by his teammate Walter Payton.

Following his retirement from professional football, Spivey settled in Colorado and pursued a career in ministry.

He maintained a close connection with his alma mater, serving as the team chaplain for the CU Buffaloes for an impressive 14 years.

Additionally, he mentored numerous student-athletes at the university.

Spivey was admired for his inspirational speaking abilities and profound understanding of scripture, earning respect for his unwavering faith and steadfast commitment to his beliefs.
